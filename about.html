History of Watches

The first portable timepieces appeared in 15th-century Europe as spring-powered clocks small enough to carry. Wristwatches gained popularity during World War I for their practicality. Today, watches range from precision mechanical movements to smart devices, yet the craft of traditional watchmaking remains as revered as ever.

Famous Brands

Rolex

Founded in 1905 in London, Rolex is synonymous with luxury and precision. Known for iconic models like the Submariner and Daytona, Rolex has set the standard for Swiss watchmaking. Every Rolex is assembled and tested by hand with meticulous attention to detail.

Omega

Omega has been at the forefront of watchmaking since 1848. It was the first watch on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission in 1969. The brand is celebrated for its Speedmaster and Seamaster lines, combining innovation with timeless elegance.

Casio

Casio revolutionized the watch industry with affordable, durable, and feature-rich timepieces. From the iconic G-Shock to the sleek Edifice line, Casio proves that great technology doesn't have to come with a luxury price tag.
